Bird Puns (2025)

My bird puns series draw on my love of birds and puns. Through this project I wanted to highlight the beauty of our native bird life and contrast it with the absurdity of how we name them.

I contrast the natural beauty of the bird with the visual language imbued in their English names and the juxtaposition is almost surreal. Lots of these names are signs and signifiers of colonial oppression (king, royal, rifle, saddle, cook’s) and some others are just light-heartedly funny (booby, tit, shag), at either end of this spectrum these words signify something completely contrary to the natural freedom and beauty of our birds.

The real absurdity is that even when the environment remains, colonialism has commandeered the names in our ecology, even when they don’t leave a clear physical mark, they leave an ideological one. Our birds are unaware of the implications of their names, providing us a visual symbol of persistence despite superficial labels. My goal with this series is though a series of visual games to get viewers to engage with knowledge of our various bird species and encourage them to think about the signifiers in those names as separate from their inherent qualities.

Lydia Tyler-Davies (2024)
